RBI Guidelines for Education Loans – Applicable to All Banks

RBI (Reserve Bank of India) guidelines that apply to education loans offered by Kredila, HDFC, SBI, ICICI and all regulated lenders.

1. IBA Model Education Loan Scheme
• All banks follow the Indian Banks' Association (IBA) Model Scheme
• Unsecured limits: Up to INR 7.5 Lakhs for recognized institutions
• Unsecured limits for premier institutes (IITs, IIMs, etc.): Up to INR 40-50 Lakhs
• Above limits: Collateral required as per bank policy

2. Interest Rate
• Floating rate linked to external benchmark (Repo, MCLR, EBR)
• Transparency: KFS (Key Fact Statement) mandatory before disbursement
• No hidden charges: Banks must disclose all fees upfront

3. Moratorium Period
• Course duration + 6 to 12 months post-employment
• Simple interest during moratorium (some banks)
• EMI starts after moratorium ends

4. Tax Benefits – Section 80E
• Interest paid on education loan deductible from income
• Available for up to 8 years from start of repayment
• Applies to loans for self, spouse, children

5. Disbursement and Documentation
• Direct disbursement to institution for fees
• Banks require: Agreement, repayment mandate (NACH), fee demand letter
• PAN-Aadhaar linking mandatory for all financial transactions
• CIBIL check before sanction and disbursement

6. Fair Practices Code (FPC)
• No discrimination; transparent communication
• Grievance redressal mechanism mandatory
• RBI Ombudsman for unresolved complaints within 30 days

7. LRS (Liberalised Remittance Scheme) for International Education
• Annual limit: $250,000 USD per person
• TCS (Tax Collected at Source): 0% for education loan funded; 5% above INR 10 Lakh for self-funded
• Form A2 for outward remittance
• Form 15CA/15CB for tax declaration on foreign remittances
